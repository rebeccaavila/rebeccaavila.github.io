Visual Design at CSUMB

Visual Design prepares students to creatively use images, symbols, typography, color, information and video to solve communication problems.

Typography

The history of type and the composition of letter forms. Creative projects include designing an alphabet, typographic gestalts, information design, and interpretive typography. Students will also introduce to page layout and typesetting skills.

Publication Design

Systematic layout of multiple page documents combining text and imagery using grid theory and information hierarchy. Readability and digital production skills are emphasized for both print and digital display delivery.

Graphic Identity

Students experience the process of designing a graphic identity system consists of a logo and its variations, stationery and graphic standards guide for actual clients.

Visual Design required courses

  • CST 351 - Web Design.
  • CST 377 - Business of Design.
  • CST 390 - Portfolio Development.
  • CST 404 - Publication Design.
  • CST 406 - Graphic Identity.
