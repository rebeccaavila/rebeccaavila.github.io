Game Design Career Exploration

There are a variety of career options for someone who is in the game design concentration. Some of the common careers include:

Career Paths

  • Game Developer
  • Visual Effects Artist
  • UX Designer
  • Art Director
  • Animator
  • Level Designer
  • Character Developer

Game Developer

Job Description:

A graphic designer is responsible for creating visual concepts, using computer software or by hand, to communicate ideas that inspire, inform, or captivate consumers.


Salary: $50,000


Skills: creativity, organization, ideation, understanding of design software, drawing, visual communication


Visual Effect Artist

Job Description:

A UX designer focuses on creating digital products that are user-friendly and easy to navigate. They conduct user research, create wireframes and prototypes, and collaborate with developers and other designers to create the final product.


Salary: $100,000


Skills: research, wireframing, visual communication, information architecture, coding


User Experience (UX) Designer

Job Description:

A UX designer focuses on creating digital products that are user-friendly and easy to navigate. They conduct user research, create wireframes and prototypes, and collaborate with developers and other designers to create the final product.


Salary: $100,000


Skills: research, wireframing, visual communication, information architecture, coding


Art Director

Job Description:

An art director is responsible for managing a team of designers and creating the visual style for a project, such as an advertising campaign, movie, or video game. They collaborate with other creative professionals, such as copywriters and photographers, to create the final product.


Salary: $80,000


Skills: creativity, vision, project management, understanding of design software, leadership


Animator

Job Description:

An animator creates animated characters and environments for films, television shows, video games, and other media. They use software to create the animation and may also work on storyboards, character design, and other aspects of the production.


Salary: $70,000


Skills: creativity, drawing, organized, analytical, attention to detail, understanding of animation software


Level Designer

Job Description:

A visual merchandiser is responsible for creating visually appealing displays in retail stores to attract customers and increase sales. They use their design skills to create eye-catching displays that showcase products and promote sales.


Salary: $40,000


Skills: creativity, sense of current design trends, communication, teamwork, understanding of design software


Character Developer

Job Description:

A brand identity designer is responsible for creating the visual elements that represent a brand, including logos, color schemes, and typography. They work closely with marketing and branding teams to create a cohesive visual identity for the brand.


Salary: $80,000


Skills: creativity, communication, organization, understanding of design software, attention to detail
